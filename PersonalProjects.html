Personal Projects

Personal projects of mine, practice works, and experiments in design and style. Many of these pieces are focused around graphic design for board and video games, alongside branding and promotional material.

Graphic Design

Card Games

As a big fan of card and board games alike I've tried my hand at making them plenty of times. Card design stands as a microcosm of graphic design challenges, pushing the limits in which one must balance between a great composition, visual hierarchy, and the viewers or users experience from any angle. This collection includes the design process and evolution of several prototype designs alongside samples of finished templates and some cards (Split Second Showdown) from a Card Game Jam in which participants had 1 week to design and playtest an entire card game based on the revealed theme.

Miscellaneous Works

Some unrelated digital design pieces and assets designed for practice or the fun of it. Alongside assets from the Liquid Crystal Collection, a collection of minigames made by me and inspired by old handheld LCD display games popular in the 90s and early 2000s.

Pixel Art

A passion of mine, one that if I allowed it, would take up this entire portfolio. A fair portion of the art I do is done within the technical bounds of old computers and game consoles, not only does this give the works a unique charm but presents an endlessly interesting design challenge. Often times limited to just 4 colors and 64x64 canvas to work with, each piece becomes a puzzle of contrast and form to be solved.

Paldea Evolved

One of stylistic practice projects, the entirety of the physical'Pokemon Trading Card Game' set "Paldea Evolved" adapted into a format matching the style of the 'Pokemon Trading card Game' released in the year 2000 for the Gameboy Color, 194 cards sporting just 4 colors and a 64x48 canvas.

Fantasy Adventure

A set of fantasy themed sprites, limited to 4 colors (all from the NES 8-bit Picture Processing Unit) and a 32x32 canvas, and sold as a licensed asset pack.
